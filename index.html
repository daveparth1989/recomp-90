03

Progression Model

RULE 01
Double Progression
Add reps until you hit the top of the rep range for ALL prescribed sets. Then add weight next session and reset to the bottom.
RULE 02
Smallest Useful Jumps
Compounds: +2.5 kg when ready. Upper-body isolations: +1.25 kg (or +1 rep twice).
RULE 03
RPE Governs Volume
If top set is RPE 9+ with form breaking, don't add. If it's RPE 6, add weight not reps.
RULE 04
Deload Protocol
Weeks 4 & 8: use ~60% of last week's working weights, halve the sets. Same movement, less load.
RULE 05
Never Skip The Main Lift
Time-crunched? Drop accessories. The first compound of each session is non-negotiable.
RULE 06
Log Everything
If it's not logged, it didn't happen. The tracker is the single source of truth — beat last week.
01

The Single Habit

01
// The one thing that makes or breaks your results
Hit your protein
every single day.

Target: 180–200 g protein/day (≈2.0–2.2 g/kg bodyweight). In a calorie deficit, protein is what prevents your 7 kg drop from being half-muscle. It's also the most satiating macro — so you spontaneously eat less junk when you hit it early.

Not training, not cardio, not supplements. If you only get one thing right for 90 days, make it this. Weigh your protein sources. Front-load at breakfast (40g+). The scale fluctuates, sleep wobbles, cardio can be missed — protein is non-negotiable.

Practical execution rules
  • ▸ Weigh in daily, average the weekly numbers on Sunday. Day-to-day is noise.
  • ▸ Sleep 7+ hours. <6 hrs doubles hunger hormones and halves fat loss. Not optional in a cut.
  • ▸ 10,000 steps/day minimum — biggest lever outside lifting.
  • ▸ Two refeed days/month at maintenance (higher carbs) to reset leptin.
  • ▸ Hydrate: 3.5 L water/day. Thirst is often misread as hunger.
  • ▸ Alcohol: ≤1 session/week, ≤3 drinks. Blunts protein synthesis 24+ hrs.
  • ▸ Creatine monohydrate: 5g/day. Highest-evidence supplement for recomps.
